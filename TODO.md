Today, our new study on AI enabled persuasion, conducted in collaboration with Oxford Internet Institute, University of Oxford, Stanford University, Massachusetts Institute of Technology and the The London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) was published in Science!

Across three experiments using 19 Large Language Models and more than 76,000 participants, we found that post-training techniques can boost persuasive power even more than model scale.

Participants held back-and-forth conversations with an LLM about one of 700 issue statements across the political spectrum, allowing us to measure how their agreement changed. We examined several drivers of persuasive capabilities in AI including model size, post-training methods and personalisation. 

Here are some of our key findings: 
üìàPost-training matters more than model scale: smaller, open-source models can match or even exceed larger closed-source models when fine-tuning techniques are applied. 
üìöInformation density has a bigger impact that personalisation: prompting models to include as many verifiable facts as possible can boost persuasiveness by 27%.
